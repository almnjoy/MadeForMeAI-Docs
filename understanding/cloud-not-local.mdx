This is the thing that trips people up most. Let's clear it up once.

## The short version

**MindMap does not run on your computer.** It runs on a private server in the cloud, a dedicated machine on the internet that you reach through your browser.

That means it cannot see:
- Files on your desktop
- Your Downloads folder
- Photos on your phone
- Anything stored only on your device
- Your browser history or open tabs

## Why this matters

When people say _"connect to my folder"_ or _"access my files,"_ they usually mean files on their own computer. MindMap can't reach those, because it's running somewhere else.

It's the same reason you can't see your home computer's files from your phone unless you set up cloud sync first. MindMap lives in a different place from your laptop.

## What you can do instead

**Upload files into the chat.** Click the attachment button in the chat window and send any file. MindMap reads it, summarizes it, pulls out data, answers questions about it. This works for PDFs, Word docs, spreadsheets, images, and more. See [File Uploads](/understanding/file-uploads).

**Connect cloud storage.** If your files already live in something like Google Drive, an admin can turn that connector on in the Admin marketplace, and MindMap can read your files from there.

**Connect email.** Once an admin enables an email connector, MindMap can read and reply to email for you. Reads are instant; sends queue for your approval.

## A helpful way to picture it

Imagine you hired a sharp assistant who works from an office across town. They're great, but they can only work with what you send them. You can email them files, give them access to your cloud storage, share a document. They can't walk into your house and open your filing cabinet.

MindMap is that assistant. It works from its own private office (the cloud) and only touches what you give it access to.

## What "the cloud" actually means

"The cloud" means computers connected to the internet and run by someone else. Gmail, Google Drive, iCloud, Dropbox, Netflix: you already use the cloud every day. MindMap is the same idea. It runs on a machine that's always on, always connected, and always available, and you reach it through your browser.

The difference is that your MindMap server is private. It's dedicated to your tenant, not shared with strangers.

A straight list of what MindMap does the moment you log in, what needs a connector turned on first, and what it cannot do yet.

## Works right now, no setup needed

These work as soon as you sign in:

**Writing and editing**
- Draft emails, messages, proposals, social posts, bios, cover letters
- Edit and improve writing you paste in (tone, length, clarity)
- Translate to other languages
- Write in a specific voice or style

**Research and information**
- Search the web and summarize what it finds
- Answer questions on any topic
- Compare options and give you a recommendation
- Summarize long documents you upload

**Planning and thinking**
- Build a week-by-week plan for a project or goal
- Help you prioritize when you have too much going on
- Think through a decision with you
- Brainstorm ideas, names, angles, strategies

**Everyday tasks**
- Draft a response to a hard email or message
- Write a review, complaint, or feedback message
- Help you figure out what to say in a tricky situation
- Put together a checklist, template, or script

**Files you upload**
- Read and summarize PDFs, Word docs, spreadsheets
- Pull key information out of documents
- Answer questions about a specific file

<Note>
Reads run instantly. Anything that changes something outside MindMap (sending an email, creating a file in a connected app) pauses and waits for your approval first. See [Tool Calls](/understanding/tool-calls).
</Note>

## Works once a connector is on

These are powerful, but an admin turns the connector on in the marketplace before MindMap can use it. The marketplace has four buckets: Apps, Tools, Skills, and Secrets. A connector usually needs a key or token saved under Secrets.

**Email** (Gmail or Microsoft 365) Read, draft, and reply to email. Ask MindMap to _"check my inbox and summarize anything important"_ or _"reply to the email from Kim about the invoice."_ Sends queue for your approval.

**Google Drive** Read, create, and organize files in your Drive. Reads are instant; anything that creates or changes a file queues for approval.

**Calendar** Check your schedule and add events. Adding an event queues for approval.

**CRM and more** Connectors for tools like a CRM, GitHub, and others show up in the marketplace too. An admin enables the ones you need.

## Things people try that don't work yet

**Reach into files on your computer** MindMap runs in the cloud, not on your laptop. It cannot see your desktop, your Downloads folder, or any local file. Upload a file into the chat and it can read it. [Why MindMap is in the cloud](/understanding/cloud-not-local)

**Place phone calls** Voice calling needs a separate connector and is off unless an admin sets it up.

## Real things people do with it every day

- _"What was that idea I had last week about [topic]?"_ It knows, because you told it.
- _"Summarize my last 10 emails and tell me what needs a response."_ Works once email is connected.
- _"I have a job interview tomorrow at [company]. Help me prepare."_ Web research plus coaching in one shot.
- _"Write a response to this negative review."_ Paste the review, get a draft in seconds.
- _"Build me a content calendar for my Instagram for the next month."_ Done in a couple of minutes.
- _"I have $500 to spend on ads. Walk me through how to use it."_ Strategy and step-by-step guidance.

---

The fastest way to learn what MindMap can do is to ask it. Start with _"what can you help me with?"_ and go from there.

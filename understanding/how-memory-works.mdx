---
title: "How Memory Works"
description: "Why MindMap remembers you across every conversation, what it saves, and how to teach it or correct it."
---

Most AI tools forget everything the second you close the tab. Every session starts from zero. MindMap does not work that way.

MindMap has persistent memory. It builds a picture of who you are over time and uses that context every time you talk to it.

## What it remembers

MindMap saves things you tell it on purpose, and it picks up patterns from your conversations over time. For example:

- Your name, what you do, where you live
- Your preferences ("I like bullet points, not walls of text")
- Your ongoing projects and goals
- People, places, and things you mention often
- How you communicate and the tone you prefer
- Deadlines and plans you've talked about

## How to teach it things

Tell it something directly and it saves it:

- _"Remember that my business partner is named Kim."_
- _"I prefer short, direct answers, no fluff."_
- _"My biggest goal this year is to get 20 new clients by December."_

You can also talk to it the way you'd talk to a person. Mention your kids, your business, your schedule, and it picks up on those things and builds context on its own.

## What it doesn't do on its own

Memory has limits. MindMap won't know something unless you told it or it came up in a conversation. A few things to keep in mind:

- **It doesn't read your email or calendar unless a connector is on.** An admin turns those on in the Admin marketplace. [What MindMap can do](/understanding/what-it-can-do)
- **It doesn't know what's on your computer.** A file on your desktop doesn't exist for MindMap until you upload it. [Why MindMap is in the cloud](/understanding/cloud-not-local)

## Fixing something it remembered wrong

Correct it the way you'd correct a colleague:

_"Actually, my business is in Dallas now, not Austin. Update that."_

_"I changed my mind on that. I'd rather get longer, detailed answers now."_

MindMap updates what it knows.

## Memory across different chats

MindMap keeps one memory that carries across all your conversations. Start a brand-new conversation and it's the same assistant with the same history.

If you also reach MindMap through a connected messaging channel, memory still flows to the same place. One assistant, one memory.

---

Tell MindMap something once and it holds onto it. The more context you give it, the more useful it gets.

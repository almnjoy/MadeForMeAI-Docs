---
title: "It Paused. What's a Tool Call?"
description: "Why MindMap sometimes pauses mid-response to show you what it is doing, and why that is a good sign."
---

Sometimes you ask MindMap something and it doesn't reply right away. It pauses for a moment and shows you something like:

> _Searching the web..._
> _Reading file..._
> _Creating document..._

That's a **tool call**. It means MindMap is doing something, not just talking.

## This is a good sign

When MindMap uses a tool, it's taking action. It searched the web. It opened a file. It drafted an email. It's doing real work instead of generating text from memory.

Picture watching someone type at a keyboard. The pause is them working, not them stuck.

## How long does it take?

Usually a few seconds. A bigger job, like searching several sources or working through a large file, might take 10 to 30 seconds. That's normal.

If a minute passes and nothing has happened, refresh the page and ask again.

## What kinds of tools does it use?

- **Web search** finds current information on the internet
- **File reading** opens a document you uploaded or one in your connected storage
- **Email** reads or sends mail through a connected email account
- **File creation** writes a document, spreadsheet, or note to your workspace
- **Calendar** checks or adds events when a calendar connector is on

## Reads vs. writes, and the approval gate

There are two kinds of tool calls, and MindMap treats them differently.

**Reads run instantly.** Searching the web, reading a file, checking your inbox: nothing changes outside MindMap, so it goes ahead.

**Writes wait for you.** Sending an email, creating a file, changing an event: MindMap pauses and shows you exactly what it's about to do before it does it.

<Note>
The approval gate is core to MindMap. Every action that changes something queues for your review first. Nothing leaves your hands without you saying yes.
</Note>

## It asked me to approve something. Do I have to?

Yes, that's the gate doing its job. Read what MindMap is about to do, and if it looks right, tell it to go ahead: _"Yes, send it"_ or _"looks good, do it."_

If you don't want it to proceed, say _"no"_ or _"stop,"_ or describe what you'd rather it do instead.

## It's not broken

The first time people see a tool call, they often think something went wrong. It didn't. MindMap is working the way it should. Give it a moment.

Your calendar connects through your automations, not a calendar that lives only inside MindMap. Wire up n8n, Zapier, or Composio to your actual calendar (Outlook, Google, cal.com, or whatever you use) and the agent can list what is coming up and add events for you. Reading your events runs immediately. Adding an event is a write, so the agent queues it and it only goes on the calendar after you approve.

## HOW TO SETUP

Connect your calendar through an automation, then point MindMap at it.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pick your automation">
    Use n8n, Zapier, or Composio, whichever you already run.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect your calendar">
    Authorize your real calendar (Outlook, Google, cal.com, or another) inside that tool.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Let the agent use it">
    Once the automation is live, ask the agent about your schedule and it reads and adds events through that connection.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## WHAT USER NEEDS TO GET

- An automation account (n8n, Zapier, or Composio).
- Access to the calendar you want to use (Outlook, Google, cal.com, etc.).

## HOW IT WORKS

- The agent lists your upcoming events when you ask. This is a read, so it runs immediately.
- Adding an event is a write. The agent queues a proposal with the date, time, title, and any
  location you gave, and it only fires after you approve. The agent never adds anything on its own.
- The events live in your real calendar through the automation you connected, not inside MindMap.

## WHAT CAN BE CALLED VIA AI

- "what's on my calendar" (read, runs immediately)
- "what do I have coming up this week" (read, runs immediately)
- "add a meeting Thursday at 3pm called Vendor call" (write, queued for approval)
- "put a dentist appointment on Friday morning" (write, queued for approval)
- "add an event Monday at 10am called Site visit at 400 Main St" (write, queued for approval)

The agent reads your events on its own and queues every new event for you to approve.

## LIMITATIONS

- It depends on the automation you connect. If that connection is down, the agent cannot read or add events.
- What the agent can do depends on what your automation exposes (invites, reminders, recurring series, and availability checks vary by setup).

## FAQ

**Which calendars can I use?** Whatever your automation connects to, such as Outlook, Microsoft 365,
Google, or cal.com. The agent works through that connection.

**Can the agent add an event without asking me?** No. Reading your events runs on its own, but every
new event queues for your approval and only goes on the calendar after you approve it.

**What do I need to set up?** An automation (n8n, Zapier, or Composio) connected to your real
calendar. Once that is live, just ask the agent about your schedule.

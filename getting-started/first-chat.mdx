Chat is the fastest way to start. Sign in at your tenant URL, open the Chat tab, and type. On a phone, Chat is already the surface you land on.

{/* MEDIA gif: user types a question, a tool-call chip appears and the run pauses, then the answer streams in word by word with a result card | caption: "Ask, watch the tools run, read the answer as it streams." */}

## Ask in plain language

You do not need commands or special prompts. Plain sentences work. Give the agent something specific to do and it will run with it.

Try one of these:

- _"What's my newest lead?"_
- _"Chart last quarter's revenue."_
- _"Make a one-page brief on this client: [paste a name or notes]."_
- _"Summarize my unread email from this week."_

The vaguer the ask, the vaguer the answer. "Help me with my business" gives you little. "Draft a follow-up email to the client who went quiet two weeks ago" gives you something you can use.

## Tool-call chips and the pause

As the agent works, small chips appear under the reply showing which tools it used. This is the agent showing its work, not a loading spinner.

Watch for the pause. MindMap runs on an approval gate:

- **Your first read runs instantly.** Ask _"what's my newest lead?"_ and the agent looks it up and answers. Nothing to approve.
- **Your first write stops and waits.** Ask _"draft and send a follow-up to them"_ and the agent prepares the action, then queues it. You see exactly what it wants to do before anything happens.

<Warning>
The agent never sends or changes anything on its own. Every write waits for you to approve it. Reads run on their own.
</Warning>

Approve the queued action and it fires. Reject it and nothing happens. When you trust an action type enough to skip the prompt, set a Trust Rule so it runs by itself next time.

## Follow-up chips

After a reply, the agent suggests a likely next step as a chip you can click instead of typing. Ask for last quarter's revenue, and a chip might offer to chart it or compare it to the quarter before. Click the chip, or ignore it and type your own thing.

Results render as inline cards. A chart, a document, a search, a lookup. Click a card to open it in a wider side panel.

## Clear and persist

Your conversation stays put when you move to the Canvas or another tab and come back.

- **Persist:** Chat history is saved in the browser you are using. Leave and return, and it is still there.
- **Clear:** Wipes the current conversation. This is permanent, with no undo.

<Note>
History is per browser. Open Chat on another device or in a different browser and you start fresh. There is no synced cross-device history.
</Note>

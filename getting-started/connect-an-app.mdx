MindMap reaches your other tools through connectors. You turn them on in the **Admin marketplace**, not by chatting with a setup wizard. Add a key or a URL to a connector card, save it, and the agent can use that tool right away.

{/* MEDIA image: an App card in the Admin marketplace, expanded to show its setup fields (API key, URL) and a Save button | caption: "Each App card has its own setup fields. Fill them in and Save." */}

## The marketplace has four buckets

Open **Admin**, then **Marketplace**. Connectors and tools are sorted into four buckets:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Apps">
    Surfaces and big integrations you switch on, like Chat, the Morning Brief, or a connector hub.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Tools">
    Individual capabilities the agent can call, like web search or a specific service.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Skills">
    Multi-step flows the agent runs on request, like Deep Research or Lead Outreach.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Secrets">
    The keys and tokens your connectors need, stored once and reused.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Turn on a connector

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Admin marketplace">
    Go to **Admin**, then **Marketplace**, then the **Apps** bucket.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick a card">
    Find the connector you want and open its card. The card lists what the connector unlocks and the fields it needs.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add its key or URL">
    Paste the API key, token, or URL the connector asks for. A card that needs a value shows a "needs key" state until you fill it in.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. The connector switches on and the agent can use it on your next message.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## One connector, many services

You do not wire up every service one at a time. A connector hub like **Composio** or **Zapier** brings a long list of services behind a single key. Add the hub once, and the agent can reach the services it covers. That keeps the marketplace short and your setup quick.

## Reads run now, writes wait for you

This is the rule for every connector:

- **Reads run instantly.** Ask the agent to look something up, list, or search, and it does so on the spot.
- **Writes queue for approval.** Anything that changes data, sends a message, or creates a record gets proposed to you first. You see exactly what it will do, then approve or reject it. Nothing leaves MindMap until you say yes.

<Note>Reads are safe to let run. Writes always pause at the approval gate, so a connector cannot send or change anything without your sign-off.</Note>

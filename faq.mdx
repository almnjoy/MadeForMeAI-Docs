<AccordionGroup>

  <Accordion title="Do I need to know anything about AI to use this?">
    No. If you can text a friend or send an email, you can use MindMap. Type what you need in plain words, like you are talking to a person. Nothing technical to set up or figure out.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is MindMap a search engine or an assistant?">
    An assistant. A search engine hands you a list of links and forgets you the moment you close the tab. MindMap is your own agent. It remembers your context, uses the tools you connect, and does the work, not the looking-up. Ask it to draft an email or pull your pipeline and it acts, instead of pointing you at ten blue links.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Does it remember me?">
    Yes. The more you use MindMap, the more it understands how you work and what you care about. You can also tell it things directly, like "my name is Sarah, I run a bakery, and I hate writing emails," and it keeps that going forward. Your memory is yours and stays with your workspace.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="It paused and said something about a 'tool' — is that normal?">
    Yes. When the agent does something active (searching the web, reading your CRM, drafting a file) it shows you the tool it is using. That is the work happening, not a bug. Give it a few seconds and the result comes back. [More on tool calls →](/understanding/tool-calls)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is the approval gate?">
    MindMap splits everything into reads and writes. Reads (listing, searching, fetching, looking something up) run the moment you ask. Writes (sending an email, posting a message, creating a record, changing data) queue for your approval. The agent shows you the exact action and waits. Nothing leaves your workspace or changes until you click approve.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can it send things without asking me?">
    No. Every write queues for your approval first. The agent never sends, deletes, or changes anything on its own. If there is one specific action you repeat and trust, you can set a Trust Rule that pre-approves a narrow version of it, so you stop clicking approve for that one case. You set that up yourself, and it stays narrow.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I add an app?">
    In the Admin marketplace. Open the App's card, flip Enable on, paste any key or URL it needs, and Save. It works on your next message, no restart. The marketplace has four buckets: Apps (outside services like your CRM or Telegram), Tools (built-in abilities like web search), Skills (reusable playbooks), and Secrets (your keys). [Marketplace overview →](/marketplace/overview)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is my data private?">
    Yes. Your conversations, files, and memory stay in your own workspace. Nothing you share gets used to train models or read by us. Your keys are stored write-only, never shown back to the browser after you save them, and never sitting in our code.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Where does MindMap run, in the cloud or on my computer?">
    In the cloud. The agent cannot see your desktop, Downloads folder, or local files unless you upload them into the chat. This trips people up at first. If you want the agent to work with a file, drop it into the conversation. [Why cloud, not local →](/understanding/cloud-not-local)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I use it on my phone?">
    Yes. MindMap works in your phone's browser, no app to download. Chat is the default view on phones. Open your tenant URL in Safari or Chrome and add it to your home screen so it opens like a real app. [Install on your phone →](/getting-started/add-to-phone)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I log in?">
    Through your organization's single sign-on at your MindMap tenant URL. You sign in with the account your administrator set up. No separate password to manage for MindMap itself.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What can it actually do?">
    Draft emails, write documents, search the web and summarize what it finds, read your CRM and move deals, plan a week or a launch, analyze files you upload, keep notes, and run saved playbooks. The reach grows with the apps you connect in the marketplace. Reads run instantly; anything that sends or changes waits for your approval. [See examples →](/understanding/what-it-can-do)
  </Accordion>

</AccordionGroup>

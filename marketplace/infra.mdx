## Overview

Infrastructure Monitoring turns your workspace into a live operations view of the servers and services you run. It combines standard open tooling such as Grafana, Prometheus, Loki, and Alloy into pages and dashboards you can read at a glance and ask your agent about in plain language.

It is set up per deployment for operators who want their infrastructure surfaced next to the rest of their work. On a standard setup it stays off; it is turned on and pointed at your own stack as part of a managed or white-glove rollout.

## What it can show

- **Live node health** — CPU, memory, disk, and load per host, as cards you can scan.
- **Dashboards** — your existing Grafana dashboards embedded inline as tiles.
- **Logs** — search recent logs across your services without leaving the page.
- **Alerts** — see and adjust alert rules; changes are approval-gated like any other write.
- **Ask in plain language** — "how is CPU on the main node", "any errors in the last hour", "show the overview dashboard". Reads run instantly and the agent answers from your live metrics and logs.

## How it works

Reads are read-only. Metrics, logs, and dashboards come straight back. The only writes are alert-rule changes, and those queue for your approval. Each deployment points at its own monitoring stack, so what you see is always your own infrastructure and nothing is shared across setups.

## Good to know

- This is an operator feature, configured for your environment rather than self-serve.
- It surfaces the stack you already run (Grafana, Prometheus, Loki, Alloy); it does not replace them.
- Available on managed and white-glove setups. Ask if you want it enabled for yours.

Look up items in your 1Password vault from the Brain Map chat, and reveal a single field value behind an approval. The search side only ever shows item titles and the field labels (what exists), never the values. To see an actual value, the agent queues an approval; you approve, and the value is shown once on the tile for copying and is never written to the audit log.

## What you need

- A 1Password service-account token (`OP_SERVICE_ACCOUNT_TOKEN`), scoped to one vault, read access. Treat it like any vault credential.
- The vault name (`OP_VAULT`) the token can read.

The container runs the official `op` CLI; the token and vault are injected per call from the marketplace key store.

## Setup

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a service account">
    In 1Password, create a service account (Developer, then Service Accounts) and grant it read access to the one vault you want Brain Map to use. Copy its token.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the 1Password card">
    Open Admin (marketplace), then the 1Password card.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste the token and vault">
    Paste the service-account token into its write-only box and Save. Paste the vault name into the Vault box and Save (the token is scoped to one vault, and item reads require naming it).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable">
    Flip Enable on. It applies on your next message, no restart.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How it works

- `op_lookup` lists items whose title matches your search and, for the top matches, the field labels they carry (password, username, card number, etc). No values.
- `reveal_secret` queues an approval for one field of one item. Nothing is revealed yet.
- On approve, the value is fetched and shown once on the tile for copying. The audit log records that a reveal happened (item plus field plus "revealed"), never the value itself.
- Common phrasings map to real field labels (e.g. "cvv" to verification number, "exp" to expiry date).
- Token and vault are read live via the marketplace key store (saved to your pod, never shown back, never in the repo).

## What you can ask

- "find the Amazon login in 1password" (lists the item plus its field labels)
- "what's in 1password for the company card" (item plus labels, no values)
- "reveal the password for the router admin" (queues an approval; on approve, shown once)
- "show the cvv on the company card" (same, approval-gated)

The agent never prints or guesses a secret value, and never claims a reveal happened before you approve it.

## Limitations

- One vault. The service-account token is scoped to a single vault.
- Reveal is one field at a time, behind an approval, shown once. There is no bulk export.
- Labels, not values, on search. `op_lookup` deliberately never returns values.
- Needs the `op` CLI in the image (it ships in the api container).

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can the agent dump my whole vault?">
    No. Search returns titles and field labels only. Values come one field at a time, each behind its own approval, and are shown once.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is the revealed value logged?">
    No. The audit log records that item X field Y was revealed, never the value.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is my token safe?">
    The key box is write-only. The token is saved to your pod, never shown back or returned to the browser, and never in the repo.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Why do I need the vault name?">
    A service-account token is scoped to one vault, and 1Password requires naming it on item reads. Without it, lookups come back empty.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I use my whole 1Password account?">
    Use a service account scoped to just the vault you want Brain Map to touch. Do not give it your personal account credentials.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

Read your Twenty CRM pipeline from the MindMap chat and move deals between stages, plus a
**Sales pipeline kanban page** you drag cards on. One toggle gives you both the agent tools
and the page. It talks straight to Twenty's API, no automation layer in between. Creating
leads and sending email are a different bundle (n8n Automations), this one is your live
pipeline, read and move-stage.

## HOW TO SETUP

1. Open MindMap, go to **Admin** (the marketplace).
2. Click the **Twenty CRM** card.
3. Paste your **Twenty API URL** and **Twenty API key** into the write-only boxes and hit
   **Save** on each.
4. Flip **Enable** on.

It applies on your next message, no restart. Enabling it also turns on the **Sales** page in
the left nav.

## WHAT USER NEEDS TO GET

- **A Twenty API key.** In Twenty: Settings → **API & Webhooks** → generate an API key. It's a
  long token (a JWT).
- **Your Twenty API URL.** The base URL of your Twenty instance, e.g.
  `https://crm.yourcompany.com` (self-hosted) or your Twenty Cloud workspace URL. MindMap
  calls `<url>/graphql`.

That's it. No connector, no agent, no dashboard to build.

## HOW IT WORKS

- MindMap calls Twenty's **GraphQL API** directly with your key. The key stays in your pod,
  never in the browser or the repo.
- It reads Twenty's **standard objects**: Opportunities (name, stage, amount, dates), the
  People who are points of contact, and Companies. Every default Twenty workspace has these.
- **Stages are auto-detected.** It reads your workspace's stage names, you don't configure
  anything. Whatever you named your stages, they show up.
- The **Sales page** is MindMap's own kanban. It reads your pipeline and, when you drag a
  card to a new column, writes that stage change straight to Twenty (a GraphQL mutation). The
  agent can do the same move, but the agent's version waits for your approval first.
- No n8n anywhere in this feature. Lead intake and templated email (which do use n8n) are the
  separate Automations bundle.

## WHAT CAN BE CALLED VIA AI

- "show my sales pipeline" / "what's in the pipeline": live pipeline by stage
- "find the lead for `<name / company / email>`": locate a deal
- "move `<deal>` to `<stage>`": queues an approval, then writes the stage to Twenty

(The Sales page does the read + drag-to-move visually, same data.)

## LIMITATIONS

- **Standard Twenty schema.** Works out of the box on a default Twenty (Opportunities / People
  / Companies). If someone deleted or renamed those standard objects or their fields, the
  queries would come back empty and need adjusting.
- **Twenty version.** The field and mutation shapes were verified on a current Twenty (v2.8.x).
  A very different version could need a small tweak, this is the thing most likely to surface
  when a new customer connects a different build.
- **Read + move-stage only.** This feature does not create opportunities or send email, those
  are the n8n Automations bundle. The only write here is moving a stage.

## FAQ

**Does it create leads or send emails?** No. Those run through n8n and live in the Automations
feature. This one reads your pipeline and moves stages.

**Does it write to my CRM?** Only stage changes, and only the ones you make: drag a kanban card
(direct) or approve the agent's proposed move (approval-gated). Nothing else writes.

**Does it work with any Twenty setup?** Any standard Twenty, yes, just URL + key. A heavily
customized schema or a very old/new version may need a query tweak.

**Is my API key safe?** The key box is write-only. It's saved to your pod and never shown back
or returned to the browser, and it's never in the repo.

**My Twenty is behind Cloudflare.** Handled, MindMap sends a browser User-Agent so Cloudflare
doesn't block the server-side call.

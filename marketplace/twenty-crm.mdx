Connect your Twenty CRM and MindMap reads your live pipeline, your people, and your companies. One toggle gives you both the agent tools in chat and the **CRM pages** in the left nav: **Sales**, **Clients**, a full **Client-360** profile, and **Activity**. Those pages shape themselves to your own fields and stages, and with editing turned on you can change a client and act on them right from the profile. It talks straight to Twenty's API, no automation layer in between.

For the full tour of the pages, see [Your CRM and Clients](/build/crm-and-clients).

## HOW TO SETUP

1. Open MindMap, go to **Admin** (the marketplace).
2. Click the **Twenty CRM** card.
3. Paste your **Twenty API URL** and **Twenty API key** into the write-only boxes and hit
   **Save** on each.
4. Flip **Enable** on.

It applies on your next message, no restart. Enabling it also turns on the **Sales** page in
the left nav. The rest of the CRM pages and inline editing roll out per account, ask in the help desk.

## WHAT USER NEEDS TO GET

- **A Twenty API key.** In Twenty: Settings → **API & Webhooks** → generate an API key. It's a
  long token (a JWT).
- **Your Twenty API URL.** The base URL of your Twenty instance, e.g.
  `https://crm.yourcompany.com` (self-hosted) or your Twenty Cloud workspace URL. MindMap
  calls `<url>/graphql`.

That's it. No connector, no agent, no dashboard to build.

## HOW IT WORKS

- MindMap calls Twenty's **GraphQL API** directly with your key. The key stays in your pod,
  never in the browser or the repo.
- It reads Twenty's **standard objects**: Opportunities (name, stage, amount, dates), the
  People who are points of contact, and Companies. Every default Twenty workspace has these.
- **Your pages shape themselves.** MindMap reads your workspace's fields, their types, and your
  stage names, then builds the CRM pages to match. You don't configure page layouts. Rename a
  stage or add a field and the pages follow.
- The **Sales page** is MindMap's own kanban. It reads your pipeline and, when you drag a
  card to a new column, writes that stage change straight to Twenty (a GraphQL mutation). The
  agent can do the same move, but the agent's version waits for your approval first.
- **Client-360, inline editing, and actions.** With editing on, a Client-360 profile lets you
  change a field inline (it writes back to Twenty) and carry one-click action buttons. These
  are approval-gated and scoped to the fields and buttons set up for your box. See
  [Your CRM and Clients](/build/crm-and-clients).
- No n8n in the connector itself. Lead intake and templated email (which do use n8n) are the
  separate Automations bundle, and the action buttons run through that same automation.

## WHAT CAN BE CALLED VIA AI

- "show my sales pipeline" / "what's in the pipeline": live pipeline by stage
- "find the lead for `<name / company / email>`": locate a deal
- "move `<deal>` to `<stage>`": queues an approval, then writes the stage to Twenty
- "update `<client>`'s `<field>` to `<value>`": queues an approval, then writes it (editing on)

(The Sales page does the read + drag-to-move visually, same data.)

## LIMITATIONS

- **Standard Twenty schema.** Works out of the box on a default Twenty (Opportunities / People
  / Companies). If someone deleted or renamed those standard objects or their fields, the
  queries would come back empty and need adjusting.
- **Twenty version.** The field and mutation shapes were verified on a current Twenty (v2.17.x).
  A very different version could need a small tweak, this is the thing most likely to surface
  when a new customer connects a different build.
- **Editing rolls out per account.** The connector reads your pipeline out of the box and writes
  stage moves. Inline field editing, the action buttons, and the full set of CRM pages ship off
  by default and get switched on per box.

## FAQ

**Does it create leads or send emails?** The connector reads your pipeline and writes stage
moves and, with editing on, inline field changes. Lead intake and templated email run through
n8n and live in the Automations feature. The Client-360 action buttons trigger that same
automation.

**Does it write to my CRM?** Stage changes always, and with editing turned on, inline field
edits from a Client-360 profile. Every write queues for your approval (or is a drag you made
yourself on the Sales kanban). Nothing writes on its own.

**Does it work with any Twenty setup?** Any standard Twenty, yes, just URL + key. A heavily
customized schema or a very old/new version may need a query tweak.

**Is my API key safe?** The key box is write-only. It's saved to your pod and never shown back
or returned to the browser, and it's never in the repo.

**My Twenty is behind Cloudflare.** Handled, MindMap sends a browser User-Agent so Cloudflare
doesn't block the server-side call.

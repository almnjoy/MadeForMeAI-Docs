The doing layer. Where the other features read APIs directly, this bundle routes actions through your n8n flows. Right now it covers three things: send a templated email, read your calendar (M365 / cal.com), and capture a lead into Twenty. One toggle says "my n8n is wired up and powers these."

## What you need

- An n8n instance reachable from Brain Map (your `N8N_BASE_URL`).
- The flows themselves, built in n8n: a templated-email send (renders the branded template and sends via Microsoft Graph), a calendar read (M365 / cal.com), and a lead-capture (find-or-create in Twenty). For a new customer these are tweaked or rebuilt for that customer's email, CRM, and calendar.

## Setup

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the n8n Automations card">
    Go to Admin (the marketplace) and click the n8n Automations card.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set your n8n base URL">
    Set your n8n base URL in the write-only box and Save.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable">
    Flip Enable on.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The catch that makes this different from the API features: the flows have to exist in your n8n. Brain Map calls webhooks; n8n does the work. The flow names and paths line up with the routes in your tenant config (`send-templated-email`, `capture-lead`, etc.).

## How it works

- For these three things, Brain Map does not call email, calendar, or CRM-write APIs itself. It POSTs to your n8n webhook and n8n runs the flow.
- Email is approval-gated. The agent drafts an email and queues an approval card. Only when you approve does Brain Map hit the n8n `send-templated-email` webhook, which renders the branded template and sends via Graph.
- Calendar reads your real M365 / cal.com events through the n8n calendar flow.
- Lead capture posts a new lead to the n8n `capture-lead` flow, which find-or-creates it in Twenty (reading the pipeline is the Twenty CRM feature, direct API).

## What you can ask

- "draft a [template] email to [person]" (queues an approval, then n8n sends on approve)
- "what's on my calendar" (upcoming M365 / cal.com events)
- "capture this as a lead" (posts a new lead into Twenty via n8n)

## Limitations

- The bundle is the wiring, not the flows. Enabling it points Brain Map at your n8n; the flows must be built there. No n8n or no flows means these do nothing.
- Per-customer. Each customer's flows differ (their email sender, CRM, calendar), so this isn't a paste-a-key feature.

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Does it send email without asking?">
    No. Email is approval-gated. The agent drafts, you approve, then n8n sends.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Why n8n instead of calling the APIs directly?">
    Flexibility and per-customer wiring. n8n can reach almost anything, and each customer's email, CRM, and calendar setup is different. The read-only, every-customer-the-same things (pipeline, balances, metrics) are direct API.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if I don't run n8n?">
    Leave this off. The direct-API features (Twenty CRM read, Money, Infra, Meraki) don't need it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is the URL safe?">
    The box is write-only, saved to your pod, never shown back, never in the repo.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

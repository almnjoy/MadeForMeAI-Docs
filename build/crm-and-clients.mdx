Your CRM pages in MindMap are not a fixed template. They build themselves from your own CRM setup. Connect your CRM and MindMap reads your real fields, stages, and lists, then renders four pages around them: **Sales**, **Clients**, a full single-client **Client-360** profile, and **Activity**. Track "Consult Booked" or "Protocol Sent" and that is what shows up, with the right columns, sections, and filters. You can also edit a client and act on them without leaving the profile.

## HOW TO SETUP

1. Open MindMap, go to **Admin** (the marketplace).
2. Turn on the **Twenty CRM** card and save your CRM URL and key (see [Twenty CRM](/marketplace/twenty-crm)).
3. Ask in the help desk to switch on the CRM pages and, if you want it, inline editing and actions. These roll out per account.

The pages read your live CRM, so there is nothing to lay out by hand. Your setup drives what you see.

## WHAT USER NEEDS TO GET

- **A connected CRM.** The Twenty CRM card has to be on and saved first. The CRM pages read through that connection.
- **Nothing else.** MindMap derives the columns, sections, and fields from your CRM schema. You do not configure page layouts.

## HOW IT WORKS

- **The pages shape themselves to your CRM.** MindMap reads your CRM's fields and their types, then builds the pages to match. Rename a stage or add a field and the pages follow, no rebuild.
- **Sales** is your pipeline by stage, the kanban you drag cards on.
- **Clients** is the list of everyone in your book, using the columns that matter to your business.
- **Client-360** is one profile per client: their details, files, notes, and history in one place, laid out in sections that come from your own fields.
- **Activity** is the running feed of what happened, grouped by the kinds of activity you actually track.
- **Sort, filter, and group** the way you already think about your clients. Those controls come from your fields too.

## EDIT AND ACT FROM A PROFILE

- **Inline editing.** Click a field on a Client-360 profile, change it, done. The edit writes back to your CRM. Like every write in MindMap, it goes through the approval gate first.
- **Action buttons.** A client's profile can carry one-click buttons for the things you do often, like send a welcome, a project kickoff, or a check-in email. Each button runs on your own templates and your own brand, through the automation you already have connected.
- **Off by default.** Editing and actions are switched on per account. Ask in the help desk to enable them and to set which fields are editable and which buttons appear.

## WHAT CAN BE CALLED VIA AI

- "show my sales pipeline" / "what's in the pipeline": live pipeline by stage
- "pull up the profile for `<client>`": opens their Client-360
- "what's the latest activity this week": reads your Activity feed
- "update `<client>`'s status to `<value>`": queues an approval, then writes it to your CRM

Reads run the moment you ask. Anything that writes queues for your approval first.

## LIMITATIONS

- **Needs a connected CRM.** The pages read through the Twenty CRM card. No connection, no pages.
- **Rolling out.** The CRM engine, inline editing, and actions ship off by default while they roll out. Ask to have your box switched over.
- **Editing is scoped.** Only the fields set as editable can be changed inline, and only the buttons set up for your box appear. This is deliberate, so a profile cannot be edited in ways you did not intend.
- **Writes are approval-gated.** Inline edits and action buttons queue for your approval, the same as any other write.

## FAQ

**Do I have to lay these pages out?** No. They build themselves from your CRM fields. Your setup is the configuration.

**What if I rename a stage or add a field?** The pages follow your CRM. Rename or add, and it shows up without a rebuild.

**Can it change a client on its own?** No. Inline edits and action buttons queue for your approval first, like every write in MindMap.

**What do the action buttons send?** Whatever you set them to, on your own templates and brand, through your connected automation. Common ones are a welcome, a project kickoff, and a check-in email.

**Why is editing off on my box?** The engine, editing, and actions roll out per account. Ask in the help desk to switch yours on and to pick the editable fields and buttons.
